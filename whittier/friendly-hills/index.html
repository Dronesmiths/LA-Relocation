Friendly Hills

An established suburban pocket near Whittier’s southern edge, characterized by parks, larger lots in places, and a steady family-focused environment.

Overview

Friendly Hills sits on the city’s southern side and offers a more suburban, low-density experience than the compact Uptown core. The neighborhood blends older, well-maintained houses with newer subdivisions, and its streets often open onto larger green spaces and regional parks. The resulting feel is residential and calm, with a notable emphasis on outdoor recreation and family life. Local shopping and services are available within short drives, while community centers and school campuses provide regular activity for families.

Housing Types

Housing in Friendly Hills primarily comprises single-family homes, often on medium-to-large lots. Architectural styles vary from ranch and bungalow models to later suburban designs and occasional custom remodels. Many homes include private backyards suitable for gardening and play, and some properties display terraced yards or gently sloped lots reflecting the local topography. Where newer development has occurred, townhomes and smaller-lot homes provide alternatives that retain a suburban sensibility while offering slightly lower maintenance responsibilities.

Friendly Hills’ housing underscores privacy and outdoor space more than immediate walkability; yards, garages, and driveways are common features that support families with vehicles and outdoor hobbies. The stock is well-suited to long-term ownership and adaptation over time.

Neighborhood Feel

The neighborhood feels steady and family-oriented. Weekdays revolve around school runs and local errands, while weekends often center on parks, recreational leagues, and backyard gatherings. Mature landscaping and well-kept properties lend a quiet dignity to the streets, and neighbors commonly interact through school activities or organized community events. The area is known for being welcoming and practical rather than flashy, which contributes to a comfortable, lived-in quality.

Who It’s Best For

Friendly Hills suits families who prioritize private outdoor space, established schools, and a suburban pace. It’s a good fit for buyers who prefer quieter streets and larger lots over dense urban living, and for homeowners who want room for gardening, outdoor hobbies, or simple family recreation. Those who need immediate access to nightlife or high-frequency transit may prefer other parts of Whittier, but residents who value neighborhood stability and outdoor amenities will find Friendly Hills appealing.

Access & Nearby Amenities

Access to regional arteries makes commuting practical by car, while local parks and recreational areas are often within short drives or easy cycles. Shopping centers and daily services are located a short drive away, and local schools and community centers are prominent neighborhood anchors. Public transit options exist on main corridors, but many households in Friendly Hills rely on cars for longer errands. The trade-off is more private outdoor space and a quieter rhythm of life.
