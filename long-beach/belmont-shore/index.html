Belmont Shore

A lively beachfront neighborhood focused on walkability, recreation, and a strong coastal identity.

Overview

Belmont Shore sits along Long Beach’s coastal edge where life revolves around the water, the strand, and a compact commercial corridor. The neighborhood is renowned for its active outdoor culture: cyclists and joggers on the boardwalk, paddleboarders in the bay, and a steady parade of people enjoying restaurants and shops on 2nd Street. The area blends beach-town energy with a residential backbone, creating an everyday experience that is social and outdoors-oriented without feeling like a resort.

Community identity here is strongly tied to public life: beaches, marinas, and waterfront trails are the stage for much of the neighborhood’s activity. At the same time, interior blocks offer quieter residential streets where porches and small yards provide personal outdoor space. The result is a neighborhood that supports both active days and relaxed evenings.

Housing Types

Housing in Belmont Shore ranges from modest beach cottages and classic bungalows to larger, modern homes and condominium developments. Properties nearest the water are often denser or oriented toward vacation-style living, while inland blocks typically contain single-family homes on smaller lots. Many homes have been updated to maximize light, outdoor living, and coastal breezes, creating interiors that emphasize open plans and easy indoor-outdoor flow.

Because lot sizes tend to be smaller than in more suburban parts of Long Beach, outdoor space is often organized into efficient patios, roof decks, and shared courtyards. Multi-family buildings and townhomes provide alternatives for residents seeking lower-maintenance ownership or rental options close to the strand. The neighborhood’s housing mix supports a wide range of household types, from singles and couples to families who prize being near the beach.

Neighborhood Feel

Belmont Shore feels like a beach community with a strong local pulse. Weekends are active, with shoppers and diners filling the sidewalks and the waterfront hosting recreation and events. Yet side streets generally retain a quieter character; evenings bring settled calm as residents return to neighborhood routines. The commercial spine on 2nd Street encourages lingering — cafes, boutiques, and casual restaurants create a welcoming scene for neighbors to connect.

There is a casual informality to life here: beach gear tucked by front doors, neighbors walking dogs at dawn, and small local gatherings that recur through the year. The maritime setting also shapes the sensory experience—salt air, distant water sounds, and expansive views—which many residents cite as central to the neighborhood’s appeal.

Who It’s Best For

Belmont Shore is ideal for people who want a coastal lifestyle within an active, walkable neighborhood. It attracts outdoor enthusiasts who prioritize access to beaches, water sports, and biking trails, as well as residents who enjoy a lively local dining and shopping scene. Small households, professionals, and families who like being able to walk to amenities often find Belmont Shore especially appealing.

Because properties can be compact and demand for coastal addresses is high, those seeking large private yards or a quiet, suburban rhythm may prefer other Long Beach neighborhoods. Still, Belmont Shore’s combination of maritime character and neighborhood life makes it a distinctive choice for people who want the beach as part of daily living.

Access & Nearby Amenities

Belmont Shore offers easy access to local shops, cafes, and small parks within walking distance of most homes. The neighborhood is well-connected by coastal bike routes and nearby arterial roads that link to other parts of Long Beach and the South Bay. While parking and lot sizes vary, the area’s walkable core reduces the need for frequent car trips for everyday errands.

Living in Belmont Shore

Long Beach's most walkable beachside neighborhood, where coastal charm meets vibrant Second Street living.

Where Belmont Shore Fits in Long Beach

Belmont Shore sits on the southeastern edge of Long Beach, bordered by the Pacific Ocean to the south and Alamitos Bay to the east. Unlike the high-rise urban feel of Downtown Long Beach or the sprawling suburban character of Bixby Knolls, Belmont Shore offers a distinctly walkable, beach-town vibe with charming cottages, tree-lined streets, and the bustling Second Street corridor at its heart.

This neighborhood is best for those who prioritize lifestyle over square footage—people who want to walk to coffee, bike to the beach, and enjoy a tight-knit community feel while still being minutes from downtown amenities.

Housing & Architecture

Belmont Shore is characterized by its charming single-family bungalows and beach cottages, many dating back to the 1920s and 1930s. You'll find a mix of original Craftsman-style homes, Spanish Revival architecture, and modernized coastal cottages, typically on smaller lots that emphasize walkability over sprawling yards.

The neighborhood also includes some low-rise condos and townhomes, particularly closer to the beach and along the bay. Homes here tend to be more expensive per square foot than inland Long Beach neighborhoods due to the prime location and lifestyle appeal.

Ownership is the dominant feel here, with many long-term residents and families who've been in the neighborhood for decades, though there is a rental market catering to young professionals and seasonal beachgoers.

Lifestyle & Day-to-Day Living

Walkability: Belmont Shore is one of the most walkable neighborhoods in Long Beach. Second Street is the main artery, lined with boutique shops, cafes, restaurants, and bars—all within a short stroll from most homes.

Dining & Shopping: The Second Street corridor offers everything from casual brunch spots to upscale dining, vintage shops, and local boutiques. It's a vibrant, pedestrian-friendly scene that feels more like a small beach town than a big city.

Parks & Beach Access: Residents enjoy direct access to the beach, the scenic Belmont Shore bike path, and nearby Alamitos Bay for kayaking and paddleboarding. The neighborhood has a relaxed, outdoor-oriented lifestyle.

Commute & Vibe: While commuting to Downtown LA or Orange County requires a drive, many residents work remotely or locally. The vibe is active yet laid-back—think morning beach walks, weekend farmers markets, and evening strolls along the shore.

Who Belmont Shore Is Best For

  • Young Professionals: Those who value walkability, dining, and beach access over large homes.
  • Small Families: Families who prioritize community, outdoor lifestyle, and proximity to the beach.
  • Remote Workers: People who can work from home and want to live in a vibrant, walkable neighborhood.
  • Empty Nesters & Retirees: Those downsizing from larger homes but seeking an active, social lifestyle.
  • Beach Enthusiasts: Anyone who wants to live steps from the ocean and enjoy coastal living year-round.
